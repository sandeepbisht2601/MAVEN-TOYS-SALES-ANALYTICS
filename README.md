🧸 Maven Toys Sales Analytics (SQL Project)

📌 Project Overview

This project is a complete SQL-based data analysis of a fictional retail company, Maven Toys, which operates across 50 store locations in Mexico.

The goal is to solve real-world business problems using SQL and extract actionable insights related to:

- Revenue & Profit Performance
- Product & Category Analysis
- Store-Level Insights
- Inventory Health
- Trends & Growth Patterns

All queries are written in MySQL and directly answer practical business questions.

📊 Key Highlights
📈 Total Revenue: $14.44M
💰 Total Profit: $4.01M
📊 Profit Margin: 27.79%
🧾 Transactions: 829K+
🛒 Units Sold: 1.09M
💵 Avg Order Value: $17.42
🧠 Business Problems Solved

This project answers 27 real business questions, grouped into 5 major areas:

1️⃣ Overall Business Performance
Total revenue, profit & margin
Monthly & yearly trends
Best performing quarters
Revenue by day of week

2️⃣ Product & Category Analysis
Top-selling products
Highest profit margin items
Category contribution to revenue
Low-performing products

3️⃣ Store & Location Performance
Top revenue-generating stores
Revenue by city & location type
Store ranking using window functions
Above vs below average stores

4️⃣ Inventory Health Analysis
Total inventory value
Stockouts & lost revenue estimation
Days of stock remaining
Inventory distribution issues

5️⃣ Trends & Growth Insights
Month-over-Month growth (LAG)
Running total revenue
Rolling averages (trend smoothing)
Consistently growing products

🛠️ SQL Techniques Used

- Aggregate Functions (SUM, AVG, COUNT)
- GROUP BY & HAVING
- Multi-table JOINs
- Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
- Window Functions:
- RANK()
- LAG()
- SUM() OVER
- CASE Statements
- Date Functions
- Subqueries
- CROSS JOIN
- NULLIF (handling division errors)

📂 Dataset Description

The dataset simulates a retail business and includes:

- Sales Transactions
- Products & Categories
- Store Locations
- Inventory Data

🔍 Key Insights

📦 Business follows a high-volume, low-ticket model
🎄 December is the highest revenue month (seasonality impact)
🏪 Airport stores generate the highest revenue per store
🧩 Lego Bricks is the top-performing product
📉 Inventory shows both overstock & stockout issues
📊 Strong year-over-year growth trend
